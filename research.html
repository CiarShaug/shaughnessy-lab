Comparative Physiology

We explore how different aquatic organisms adapt to environmental challenges, using a comparative approach to reveal fundamental physiological mechanisms.

CFTR Function in Cystic Fibrosis

Our lab investigates the regulation and evolution of the CFTR chloride channel in both model systems and non-model vertebrates.

Thermal Tolerance in Amphibians and Fish

By studying heat and cold stress responses, we seek to understand the physiological basis of thermal tolerance and its implications under climate change.

Endocrine Regulation of Osmoregulation

We focus on hormonal signaling mechanisms that control ion transport processes critical for salt and water balance in vertebrates.
