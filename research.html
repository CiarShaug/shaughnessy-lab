ComparativePhysiology

Epithelial Ion Transportand Osmoregulation

Aquatic vertebrates must regulate salt and water transport across epithelial surfaces to survive in changing environments. This research focuses on:

  • Diversity and plasticity of ionocytes in seawater- and freshwater-acclimated fishes
  • Transporter expression (e.g., CFTR, Ano1, NKCC1) and apical-basolateral coordination
  • Endocrine regulation via corticosteroids, prolactin, and vasotocin
  • Comparative models such as sea lamprey, Atlantic sturgeon, and killifish

Representative publications: Shaughnessy et al. 2025, J Exp Biol; Breves & Shaughnessy 2024, J Comp Physiol B

Neuroendocrine Controlof Stress and Adaptation

The lab explores evolution and regulation of the hypothalamus–pituitary–interrenal (HPI) axis across basal and derived vertebrates. Topics include:

  • Functional studies of melanocortin receptors (Mc2r) and hormonal responses to stress
  • Comparative regulation of cortisol and glucose metabolism in lamprey and sturgeon
  • Environmental effects on HPI axis signaling during development and acclimation

Representative publications: Shaughnessy et al. 2025, J Mol Endocrinol; Shaughnessy & McCormick 2023, Can J Fish Aquat Sci

Evolutionof Hormone Signaling Systems

This research investigates the structural and functional evolution of endocrine signaling pathways, with emphasis on:

  • Melanocortin and CRH receptors across jawless and jawed vertebrates
  • MRAP accessory protein evolution and receptor trafficking
  • Ancestral sequence reconstruction and functional assays

Representative publications: Shaughnessy et al. 2023, Gen Comp Endocrinol; Dores et al. 2022, Biomolecules

Applied EnvironmentalPhysiology

Applied comparative studies address real-world challenges in conservation and species management:

  • Thermal tolerance in black bass hybrids (ODWC-funded)
  • Physiological constraints in species distribution models
  • Student-driven projects on stress physiology in native fishes

Representative funding: ODWC Fisheries Division (2025–2026)

TranslationalPhysiology

CFTR Functionand Regulation

CFTR is essential for epithelial fluid and ion transport. Basic research in the lab focuses on:

  • GPCR-mediated signaling pathways that activate CFTR
  • Functional phenotyping of mutant CFTR in airway models
  • Cell-type specific expression and chloride conductance properties

Representative publications: Shaughnessy et al. 2022, Am J Physiol Lung; Shaughnessy et al. 2021, Sci Rep

CF Therapeuticsand Disease Modeling

Applied research supports evaluation of modulator efficacy and drug synergy using in vitro CF models:

  • Ivacaftor, elexacaftor, and lubiprostone testing in airway cultures
  • Voltage clamp and Ussing chamber assays for functional screening
  • Development of robust models for hard-to-treat CFTR mutations

Representative publications: Shaughnessy et al. 2022, J Cyst Fibros; Bratcher et al. 2020, Physiol Rep
