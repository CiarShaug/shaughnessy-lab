ResearchThemes

EpithelialIon Transport and Osmoregulation

Aquatic vertebrates face diverse osmotic challenges across environments, and maintaining salt and water balance is essential for survival. Research in the Shaughnessy Lab investigates the molecular and physiological mechanisms underlying epithelial ion transport, with particular emphasis on:

  • Ionocyte diversity and function in freshwater- and seawater-acclimated fishes
  • Chloride channel expression and localization, including CFTR, Ano1, and NKCC1
  • Endocrine regulation by corticosteroids, prolactin, and vasotocin
  • Comparative models such as sea lamprey, Atlantic sturgeon, and killifish

Representative publications: Shaughnessy et al. 2025, J Exp Biol; Breves & Shaughnessy 2024, J Comp Physiol B

CFTR Functionin Cystic Fibrosis Models

The cystic fibrosis transmembrane conductance regulator (CFTR) is central to epithelial ion and fluid transport. Work in the Shaughnessy Lab explores how CFTR activity is regulated in human airway models, with a focus on pharmacological modulation and receptor-mediated signaling:

  • Mechanisms of CFTR activation through prostaglandin and GPCR pathways
  • Modulator efficacy and synergy, including responses to ivacaftor, elexacaftor, and lubiprostone
  • Disease modeling using primary and immortalized epithelial cultures
  • Functional screening tools for evaluating modulator sensitivity and cell-type specific responses

Representative publications: Shaughnessy et al. 2022, Am J Physiol Lung; Shaughnessy et al. 2022, J Cyst Fibros

NeuroendocrineControl of Stress and Adaptation

The lab investigates the regulation and evolution of the hypothalamus–pituitary–interrenal (HPI) axis across vertebrate lineages, focusing on:

  • Comparative function of the HPI axis in basal fishes
  • Pharmacological and molecular analysis of melanocortin receptors and accessory proteins
  • Endocrine responses to osmotic and thermal stress
  • Evolutionary perspectives on hormone-receptor coevolution

Representative publications: Shaughnessy et al. 2025, J Mol Endocrinol; Shaughnessy et al. 2024, Gen Comp Endocrinol

Evolutionof Hormone Signaling Systems

The lab studies the evolution of endocrine signaling, including receptor structure–function and ancestral reconstruction:

  • Melanocortin receptor (Mc2r) evolution and ligand selectivity
  • CRH receptor function and co-evolution with the HPI axis
  • MRAP1/MRAP2 structure and function across vertebrates
  • Use of ancestral sequence reconstruction to explore endocrine diversification

Representative publications: Shaughnessy et al. 2023, Gen Comp Endocrinol; Dores et al. 2022, Biomolecules

AppliedPhysiology and Collaborative Projects

Applied research in the Shaughnessy Lab connects physiological mechanisms with conservation, fisheries biology, and human health:

  • Thermal tolerance and climate resilience in native and invasive black bass species (ODWC-funded)
  • Species distribution modeling based on physiological constraints
  • Development of cell-based assays for CFTR modulator testing
  • Student-led investigations in lamprey and sturgeon osmoregulation

Representative funding: ODWC Fisheries Division (2025–2026); NIH F32 and NSF PRFB Fellowships
